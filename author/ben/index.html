Many places are worse than Flint when it comes to childhoood rates of high blood lead levels (BLLs)

http://www.reuters.com/investigates/special-report/usa-lead-testing/ (12/19/2016)

Summary:

  • 5% of children in Flint have high BLLs
  • 3000 areas with high BLL rates at least 2x that of Flint
  • "In some pockets of Baltimore, Cleveland, and Philadelphia, where lead poisoning has spanned generations, the rate of elevated tests over the last decade was 40 to 50 percent."
  • 2.5% of all small children nationally have BLLs higher than 5 ug/dL, the CDC's standard for intervention, unreal. 4 million households.
  • Reuters got data by census tract (or zip code) by requesting such info from states. It's not general available--usually statewide or countywide data is disclosed. They have maps for especially bad places: Baltimore, Buffalo, Cleveland, Chose City, Fresno, LA, Milwaukee, South Bend, St. Joseph.
  • Reuters got data from 21 states and ~60% of population.
  • PA is the worst state: "In 49 different tracts, from inner city Philadelphia to capital Harrisburg, at least 40 percent of children tested had high lead."
  • In many places there is very little funding for lead testing (let alone remediation)
